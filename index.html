ROBERT NESTA "BOB" MARLEY

(1945-1981)
The Legend Bob Marley: A Jamaican reggae singer, songwriter, musician, and guitarist.

A LEGEND

Timeline of Bob Marley's life:

  • 1945 - Born in Nine Mile, Saint Ann Parish, Jamaica.
  • 1955 - His father died of a heart attack at the age of 70.
  • 1957 -Left Nine Mile with his mother and moved to Trenchtown, Kingston.
  • 1962 - Recorded four songs, "Judge Not", "One Cup of Coffee", "Do You Still Love Me?" and "Terror", at Federal Studio for local music producer Leslie Kong.
  • 1963 - Bob Marley, Bunny Wailer, Peter Tosh, Junior Braithwaite, Beverley Kelso, and Cherry Smith were called The Teenagers. They later changed the name to The Wailing Rudeboys, then to The Wailing Wailers, at which point they were discovered by record producer Coxsone Dodd, and finally to The Wailers.
  • 1964 - Their single "Simmer Down" for the Coxsone label became a Jamaican #1 in february selling an estimated 70,000 copies.
  • 1966 - Braithwaite, Kelso, and Smith left The Wailers, leaving the core trio of Bob Marley, Bunny Wailer, and Peter Tosh.
  • 1966 - Married Rita Anderson, and moved near his mother's residence in Wilmington, Delaware in the United States
  • 1974 - The Wailers disbanded.
  • 1975 - Realeased "No Woman, No Cry" among others, his first hit outside Jamaica.
  • 1977 - Released Exodus which later establised is worldwide reputation and produced his status as one of the world's best-selling artists of all time.
  • July 1977 - Diagonised with a type of malignant melanoma.
  • 1979 - Released "Zimbabwe", "Africa Unite" to reflect his support for the struggles of Africans.
  • 1980 - The Album "Uprising" was released which inclues "Redemption Song" and "Forever Loving Jah" is final Studio Album, and is one of his most religious productions.
  • September 1980 - Appeared at the Stanley Theater (now called The Benedum Center for the Performing Arts) in pittsburgh, Pennsylvania; his last concert
  • May 1981 - Died at age 36: Miami Florida, United States.

He was an experience which left an indelible imprint with each encounter. Such a man cannot be erased from the mind. He is part of the collective consciousness of the nation
